Role of padding in DES. Why is padding necessary? What problems arise if padding is incorrect?

DES encrypts 64-bit (8-byte) blocks only.
Most messages are not multiples of 8 bytes.

Why padding is needed

To fill the last block so DES can encrypt it.

Common padding method

PKCS#5 (or PKCS#7)
Example:
If 3 bytes are missing, append 03 03 03.

If padding is not handled correctly

- Decryption may produce garbage or “extra characters”.

- Receiver cannot tell where real data ends.

- Padding oracle attacks become possible.

- Message integrity breaks.

Padding ensures both sides interpret the message boundary correctly.
